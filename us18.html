Ham – The First Hominid in Space

On January 31, 1961, a chimpanzee named Ham became the first hominid to travel to space aboard a Mercury-Redstone rocket. His 16.5-minute suborbital flight paved the way for human spaceflight by demonstrating survival and task performance in microgravity.

Ham’s successful mission confirmed that life-support systems and biological responses could endure spaceflight, directly influencing the decision to launch Alan Shepard a few months later.

Specifications:

Flight:Mercury-Redstone 2
Duration:16.5 min
