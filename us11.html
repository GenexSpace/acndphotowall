Hubble Space Telescope – Unveiling the Cosmos (1990–Present)

Launched aboard the Space Shuttle Discovery in 1990, the Hubble Space Telescope has revolutionized our understanding of the universe. Orbiting above Earth’s atmosphere, Hubble has provided breathtaking images of galaxies, nebulae, and supernovae with unparalleled clarity.

Equipped with a 2.4-meter mirror and multiple instruments, Hubble was serviced by astronauts on five missions, which extended its operational life and capabilities. Its discoveries have influenced nearly every branch of astronomy, from measuring the expansion rate of the universe to detecting exoplanets.

Specifications:

Launch:April 24, 1990
Launch Vehicle:Space Shuttle Discovery (STS-31)
Orbit:Low Earth Orbit (547 km altitude)
Mirror Diameter:2.4 meters (primary mirror)
Mass:11,110 kg
Instruments:WFC3, ACS, COS, STIS, NICMOS
Mission Duration:Ongoing (over 30 years in operation)
Operators:NASA / ESA
