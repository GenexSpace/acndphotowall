GSLV – Heavy-Lift Launch Vehicle

The GSLV (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle) is designed to launch heavier payloads into geostationary transfer orbits. It features a cryogenic upper stage developed indigenously by ISRO.

It supports key communication, weather, and navigation satellite missions for India and is crucial to upcoming crewed spaceflight efforts.

Specifications:

First Launch:2001 (GSLV-D1)
Stage:Cryogenic Upper Stage
Use:Heavy payload to GTO
