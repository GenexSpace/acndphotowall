SATURN V

The Saturn V rocket was developed by NASA under the direction of aerospace engineer Wernher von Braun for the Apollo and Skylab programs from 1967 until 1973. Standing 110.6 meters (363 feet) tall and weighing approximately 2,970,000 kilograms (6,540,000 pounds), it remains the tallest, heaviest, and most powerful rocket ever flown operationally.

The rocket had three stages. The first stage burned kerosene and liquid oxygen to produce immense thrust, lifting the rocket through the dense lower atmosphere. The second stage used liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen, carrying the spacecraft further into orbit. The third stage propelled astronauts out of Earth's orbit and toward the Moon.

Saturn V successfully carried astronauts to the Moon six times between 1969 and 1972, including the famous Apollo 11 mission, where Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the lunar surface. The rocket also launched Skylab, America's first space station, demonstrating its versatile capability.
