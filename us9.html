TELSTAR – FIRST COMMUNICATIONS SATELLITE (1962)

Telstar 1 was launched on July 10, 1962, by NASA aboard a Thor-Delta rocket. It was the first active communications satellite capable of relaying television signals, telephone calls, and fax images. It enabled the first live transatlantic television broadcast and marked the beginning of satellite communications. Orbital Details:
- Orbit Type: Medium Earth Orbit (MEO), elliptical
- Perigee: ~952 km
- Apogee: ~5,933 km
- Inclination: 44.8°
- Orbital Period: ~2 hours and 37 minutes
Technical Specifications:
- Mass: 77 kg
- Diameter: 0.88 m
- Power: 3,600 solar cells generating 14 W
- Transponder: 6W output power at 4 GHz
