Voyager 1 & 2 – Exploring the Outer Solar System (1977)

Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 were twin spacecraft designed to take advantage of a rare planetary alignment to perform close-up studies of Jupiter and Saturn, with Voyager 2 continuing on to Uranus and Neptune. Developed by NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), these spacecraft carried scientific instruments and a Golden Record containing sounds and images intended to portray the diversity of life and culture on Earth.

Voyager 1 entered interstellar space in 2012, becoming the first human-made object to do so, while Voyager 2 followed in 2018. Both continue to send valuable data about the heliosphere and interstellar medium, operating over 45 years after launch. The Voyager missions represent some of the most successful long-duration deep space missions in history.

Specifications:

Status:Operational
