Saturn V – The Most Powerful Rocket

The Saturn V was a multistage super heavy-lift launch vehicle developed by NASA under the Apollo program. Standing 110 meters tall, it remains the most powerful rocket ever successfully flown and was the backbone of America’s human space exploration missions to the Moon.

Between 1967 and 1973, Saturn V launched 13 missions including Apollo 11—the first manned lunar landing. Its reliability and lifting capacity played a critical role in achieving the United States’ goals in space during the Cold War era.

Specifications:

First Launch:November 9, 1967 (Apollo 4)
Final Launch:May 14, 1973 (Skylab 1)
Height:110.6 meters (363 feet)
Mass:2,970,000 kg (6.54 million lbs)
Stages:3 (S-IC, S-II, S-IVB)
Payload to LEO:140,000 kg
Payload to Moon:48,600 kg (Apollo payload)
Engines:5 F-1 (Stage 1), 5 J-2 (Stage 2), 1 J-2 (Stage 3)
Manufacturer:NASA / Boeing / North American / Douglas / IBM
Notable Missions:Apollo 11 (Moon landing), Apollo 13, Skylab
