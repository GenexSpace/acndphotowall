LONGEST FEMALE SPACEFLIGHT – SUNITA WILLIAMS

Sunita Williams, a NASA astronaut of Indian descent, held the record for the longest single spaceflight by a woman (195 days) and performed seven spacewalks totaling over 50 hours. She flew on missions aboard the Space Shuttle and Soyuz, serving on the International Space Station twice. Williams is also a U.S. Navy test pilot and continues to contribute to spaceflight programs, including NASA’s Artemis mission planning. Her journey is an inspiration for future generations, especially women in STEM.
