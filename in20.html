Nigar Shaji – Project Director of Aditya-L1

Nigar Shaji, a senior scientist at ISRO, serves as the Project Director of Aditya-L1, India’s first solar mission. With decades of experience in satellite mission management, she has played a critical role in steering the complex project aimed at studying the Sun's corona and solar weather patterns from Lagrangian Point 1.

Shaji's leadership symbolizes the rising prominence of women in India’s space program. Under her guidance, Aditya-L1 has successfully reached its operational orbit and begun transmitting valuable solar data, reinforcing India’s capabilities in heliophysics and deep-space observation.

Profile:

Full Name:Nigar Sultana Shaji
Date of Birth:1964
Nationality:Indian
Education:B.E. in Electronics & Communication (Government College of Engineering, Tirunelveli); M.Tech. in Electronics (Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra)
ISRO Career:Joined in 1987; roles at Satish Dhawan Space Centre and U R Rao Satellite Centre
Key Roles:Project Director – Aditya-L1; Associate Project Director – Resourcesat-2A
Current Position:Programme Director for Low Earth Orbit and Planetary Missions
Honors:Times Now “Amazing Indian” Award (2023); 'Eve of Excellence' by Tiruchirappalli Regional Engineering College
Personal:Lives in Bengaluru with mother and daughter; husband works in Dubai; son is a scientist in the Netherlands
