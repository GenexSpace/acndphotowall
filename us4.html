Space Shuttle Program (1981–2011)

The International Space Station (ISS) is a habitable research laboratory that orbits Earth approximately every 90 minutes. Initiated in 1998 as a collaborative project between NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, JAXA, and CSA, the ISS represents the largest peacetime scientific collaboration in human history.

It serves as a unique platform for conducting experiments in biology, physics, astronomy, and other fields in microgravity. Continuously inhabited since November 2000, the ISS symbolizes international cooperation, innovation, and the future of human presence in space.

Specifications:

Orbit:~400 km altitude
Modules:Over 15
Inhabitants:6–7 crew
Partners:NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, JAXA, CSA
