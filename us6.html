NASA’S PERSEVERANCE – MARS (2021)

Launched on July 30, 2020, and landing on Mars on February 18, 2021, NASA’s Perseverance rover explores the Jezero Crater — a location thought to be an ancient river delta. The rover is searching for signs of ancient microbial life and collecting soil samples for future return missions. Perseverance carries advanced instruments, including SHERLOC and PIXL, to analyze rock textures and chemistry. It also deployed Ingenuity, the first helicopter to fly on another planet. The mission represents a major step in NASA’s Mars Sample Return program and builds on the legacy of previous rovers such as Spirit, Opportunity, and Curiosity.
