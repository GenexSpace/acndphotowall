Chandrayaan-1 – Discovery of Water on the Moon (2008)

Chandrayaan-1, India’s first lunar mission, was launched on October 22, 2008, aboard the PSLV-C11. It carried 11 scientific instruments from India, NASA, ESA, and other space agencies, and orbited the Moon to create a 3D atlas of its surface and study its mineral composition.

One of its most significant discoveries was the detection of water molecules on the Moon’s surface, a finding confirmed by NASA's Moon Mineralogy Mapper. The mission lasted 312 days and established ISRO as a capable player in deep space exploration.

Specifications:

Launch Date:October 22, 2008
Launch Vehicle:PSLV-C11
Orbit:Lunar Polar Orbit
Mass:1380 kg
