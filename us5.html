INTERNATIONAL SPACE STATION (1998–PRESENT)

The International Space Station (ISS) is a symbol of international cooperation and scientific progress. Launched in 1998, it is a collaborative project involving NASA (USA), Roscosmos (Russia), ESA (Europe), JAXA (Japan), and CSA (Canada). Orbiting approximately 400 km above Earth, the ISS serves as a laboratory for research in microgravity, space medicine, Earth observation, and technology testing. It has hosted over 260 astronauts from 20+ nations and has played a pivotal role in preparing humans for long-duration space missions, including future missions to Mars. The station is built from modular components delivered via space shuttles and rockets. Its continued operation showcases what nations can accomplish when working together in space.
