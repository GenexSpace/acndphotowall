Rakesh Sharma – India’s First Man in Space (1984)

Wing Commander Rakesh Sharma made history on April 3, 1984, by becoming the first Indian to travel into space. He flew aboard the Soviet Soyuz T-11 spacecraft and spent nearly eight days aboard the Salyut 7 space station. During the mission, he conducted scientific experiments and Earth observations as part of the Indo-Soviet space collaboration.

His legendary reply to then Prime Minister Indira Gandhi, “Saare Jahan Se Achha,” when asked how India looked from space, remains etched in national memory. Sharma's achievement became a source of immense pride and inspired future generations to pursue space science and aviation.

Specifications:

Mission Date:April 3–11, 1984
Spacecraft:Soyuz T-11
Partner:Soviet Interkosmos Program
Time in Space:7 days, 21 hours
Experiments:Remote sensing, material science, life sciences
Notable Quote:“Saare Jahan Se Achha”
