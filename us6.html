NASA’s Perseverance – Mars Rover

NASA’s Perseverance rover was launched on July 30, 2020, and landed on Mars in the Jezero Crater on February 18, 2021. It is a part of NASA's Mars 2020 mission and serves as the most advanced robotic explorer ever sent to the Red Planet. Built by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), Perseverance is tasked with seeking signs of ancient life and collecting samples for future return to Earth.

Equipped with cutting-edge scientific instruments and cameras, Perseverance is also testing new technologies, including the Ingenuity helicopter—the first aircraft to fly on another planet. This mission represents a vital step toward future human exploration of Mars, contributing data on the environment, geology, and potential habitability of Mars.

Specifications:

Landing Date:Feb 18, 2021
Landing Site:Jezero Crater
Launch Vehicle:Atlas V 541
Mass:1025 kg
Instruments:23 cameras, Ingenuity, MOXIE, SHERLOC, PIXL
