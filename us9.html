Telstar – First Communications Satellite (1962)

Telstar 1, launched on July 10, 1962, was the world’s first active communications satellite capable of relaying television, telephone, and fax signals. It marked a pivotal moment in global telecommunications by transmitting the first live television images between continents.

Developed by Bell Telephone Laboratories for AT&T and launched aboard a Thor-Delta rocket, Telstar orbited the Earth every 2.5 hours and demonstrated the feasibility of satellite communications, laying the foundation for the worldwide communications infrastructure that followed.

Specifications:

Launch Date:July 10, 1962
Launch Vehicle:Thor-Delta
Mass:77 kg
Orbit:Highly Elliptical (952–5,930 km)
Orbit Period:2 hours and 37 minutes
Function:First live transatlantic TV broadcast
Operator:AT&T / NASA collaboration
Status:Deactivated in February 1963
