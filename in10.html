Aditya-L1 – India’s First Solar Mission (2023)

Aditya-L1 is India’s first dedicated solar mission, launched on September 2, 2023, by ISRO. It is designed to study the Sun’s corona, solar flares, magnetic field variations, and space weather phenomena. Positioned at the Lagrangian Point 1 (L1), it allows uninterrupted observation of the Sun without Earth eclipses.

Carrying seven scientific payloads including SUIT, VELC, and SoLEXS, Aditya-L1 aims to enhance our understanding of solar dynamics and their influence on the Earth’s climate and near-space environment. It marks a significant advancement in India's heliophysics research capabilities.

Specifications:

Launch Date:September 2, 2023
Launch Vehicle:PSLV-XL (C57)
Launch Site:Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota
Mission Type:Solar Observatory
Orbit:Halo orbit around Sun–Earth Lagrange Point 1 (L1), ~1.5 million km from Earth
Spacecraft Mass:1,475 kg
Mission Duration:Planned: 5 years
Payloads:7 instruments (4 remote sensing, 3 in-situ)
Key Instruments:VELC, SUIT, ASPEX, PAPA, SoLEXS, HEL1OS, Magnetometer
Scientific Goals:Study solar corona, solar wind, CMEs, and space weather dynamics
