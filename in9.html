INSAT – TRANSFORMING INDIA’S COMMUNICATION (1983–PRESENT)

The Indian National Satellite System (INSAT) is a series of multipurpose geostationary satellites used for telecommunications, broadcasting, weather forecasting, and disaster warning. Since its inception in 1983, INSAT has enabled mobile and satellite TV, cyclone tracking, and rural connectivity, transforming the socio-economic landscape of India.
