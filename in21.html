ISRO’s Record-Breaking Launch – 104 Satellites in One Mission (2017)

On February 15, 2017, ISRO set a world record by launching 104 satellites in a single mission aboard the PSLV-C37 rocket. This historic launch included India’s Cartosat-2D and 103 co-passenger nanosatellites from countries like the United States, Israel, Kazakhstan, Netherlands, and Switzerland.

This achievement showcased ISRO’s growing prowess in precision multi-payload deployment and orbital coordination. It significantly boosted India’s image in the commercial satellite launch market and demonstrated the reliability of the PSLV platform for rideshare missions.

Specifications:

Launch Date:February 15, 2017
Launch Vehicle:PSLV-C37
Primary Payload:Cartosat-2D
Total Satellites:104 (101 foreign + 3 Indian)
Customer Nations:USA, Israel, Netherlands, Switzerland, UAE, etc.
Record:Most satellites launched in a single mission (2017)
