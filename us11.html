HUBBLE SPACE TELESCOPE (1990–PRESENT)

Deployed in 1990 by the Space Shuttle Discovery, the Hubble Space Telescope orbits Earth at about 547 km altitude. Its instruments capture visible, ultraviolet, and near-infrared light. Hubble has revealed insights into galaxy formation, dark matter, black holes, and the age of the universe. Its iconic images — like the Pillars of Creation and Deep Field — have transformed both science and public imagination. It remains in service and works in tandem with newer observatories like the James Webb Space Telescope.
