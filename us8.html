JAMES WEBB SPACE TELESCOPE (2021)

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is NASA's most advanced space observatory, launched on December 25, 2021. It orbits the Sun at Lagrange Point 2 (L2), nearly 1.5 million kilometers from Earth. JWST is equipped with a large 6.5-meter mirror and powerful infrared instruments to peer into the earliest stages of the universe. It studies galaxy formation, black holes, exoplanets, and the atmospheres of distant worlds — potentially identifying signs of life. JWST is considered the successor to the Hubble Space Telescope and has already captured stunning images that redefine our cosmic perspective.
