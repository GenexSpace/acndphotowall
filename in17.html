PSLV – India’s Workhorse Rocket

The Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) has earned a reputation as one of the most reliable launch vehicles in the world. First launched in 1993, PSLV has been pivotal in deploying Indian and foreign satellites into Sun-Synchronous and polar orbits. Its versatility allows it to carry payloads ranging from small CubeSats to large Earth observation satellites.

With over 50 successful missions, PSLV has demonstrated multi-payload capability and consistent performance. It was used to launch Chandrayaan-1, Mangalyaan, and the record-setting 104 satellites on a single mission (PSLV-C37) in 2017.

Specifications:

First Flight:September 20, 1993
Max Payload to SSO:1,750 kg
Stages:Four (alternating solid and liquid)
Notable Missions:Chandrayaan-1, Mangalyaan, PSLV-C37
Variants:PSLV-G, PSLV-XL, PSLV-CA
Reliability:Over 50 successful launches
