SALLY RIDE – FIRST AMERICAN WOMAN IN SPACE (1983)

In 1983, Sally Ride made history by becoming the first American woman to travel to space. She flew aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger during the STS-7 mission, where she operated the shuttle’s robotic arm to deploy satellites.

A physicist by training, Ride broke barriers in a field long dominated by men, inspiring generations of young women to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).

After her career at NASA, she co-founded Sally Ride Science to promote STEM education. Her work as an astronaut, educator, and advocate has left a lasting impact on the space community and education systems across the world.

Specifications and Facts:

Flight Date:June 18–24, 1983
Mission:STS-7 (Space Shuttle Challenger)
Role:Mission Specialist
Legacy:First American woman in space, STEM advocate
Post-NASA:Founder of Sally Ride Science
