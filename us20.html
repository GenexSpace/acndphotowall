Wernher von Braun – Rocket Visionary

Wernher von Braun was a pioneering rocket engineer who played a leading role in the development of Nazi Germany’s V-2 rocket and later the U.S. Saturn V. After World War II, he was brought to the U.S. through Operation Paperclip and became instrumental in America’s space program.

As director of NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center, von Braun led the development of Saturn V, which powered the Apollo missions. His vision and engineering legacy continue to influence space exploration to this day.
