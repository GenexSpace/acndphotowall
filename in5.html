Gaganyaan – India’s First Human Spaceflight Program (Upcoming 2025)

The Gaganyaan mission is India's ambitious human spaceflight program, with a crewed launch scheduled for 2025. Developed by ISRO, the mission aims to send a three-member crew into Low Earth Orbit for a mission duration of up to 7 days. The fully autonomous Gaganyaan Crew Module will provide life support, navigation, and safety systems tailored for human travel.

It marks India’s entry into the exclusive group of nations capable of independent human space travel. The program also fosters collaborations with national and international partners, setting the stage for long-term crewed missions in the future.

Specifications:

Mission Name:Gaganyaan
Planned Launch:2025 (crewed)
Launch Vehicle:LVM3 (GSLV Mk III)
Launch Site:Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota
Mission Type:Crewed Low Earth Orbit Mission
Crew Capacity:Up to 3 astronauts
Orbit Altitude:300–400 km
Mission Duration:Up to 7 days
Spacecraft Mass:~5,300 kg (Crew Module)
Support Module:Service Module with life support and propulsion
Status:Uncrewed test flights in preparation (G1, G2)
