Sally Ride – First American Woman in Space

On June 18, 1983, Dr. Sally Ride became the first American woman in space aboard Space Shuttle Challenger’s STS-7 mission. A physicist and astronaut, Ride shattered gender barriers in the U.S. space program and became a national icon.

She flew on two space missions and later devoted herself to science education and outreach, founding Sally Ride Science to encourage young people, particularly girls, to pursue STEM careers.
