RAKESH SHARMA – INDIA’S FIRST MAN IN SPACE (1984)

Rakesh Sharma became the first Indian in space aboard the Soviet Soyuz T-11 in 1984.

He spent 8 days aboard the Salyut 7 space station and conducted scientific experiments. His famous quote about India from space, “Saare Jahan Se Achha,” inspired generations.

Mission Highlights:


- Launch Date: April 2, 1984


- Duration: 7 days, 21 hours


- Experiments: Material and life sciences


- Partner Agency: Soviet Intercosmos


Mission Summary:

  • Spacecraft: Soyuz T-11
  • Duration in space: ~8 days
  • Altitude: ~350 km orbit (Salyut 7)
  • Backup Crew: Ravish Malhotra
