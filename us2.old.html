International Space Station (ISS)

The International Space Station (ISS) is a modular space station in low Earth orbit. It serves as a microgravity and space environment research laboratory where scientific research is conducted in astrobiology, astronomy, meteorology, and physics.

The ISS is a joint project of NASA, Roscosmos, JAXA, ESA, and CSA and has been continuously occupied since November 2000, making it one of the longest-running space missions in human history.

Specifications:

Launch Date:November 20, 1998
Partners:NASA, Roscosmos, JAXA, ESA, CSA
Orbit:Low Earth Orbit (LEO)
Altitude:~420 km
Speed:~28,000 km/h
Modules:16 Pressurized Modules
