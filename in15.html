SSLV – India’s Small Satellite Launch Vehicle (2022)

The Small Satellite Launch Vehicle (SSLV) is a compact and low-cost launch system developed by ISRO to meet the growing demand for launching small payloads into Low Earth Orbit (LEO). Designed for rapid assembly and integration, SSLV can carry up to 500 kg of payload to LEO and supports on-demand launch services for commercial and academic users.

Its maiden developmental flight occurred on August 7, 2022. Although it experienced a partial failure, the second test flight on February 10, 2023, successfully placed satellites into their intended orbits, marking a new chapter in India’s small satellite deployment capability.

Specifications:

First Launch:August 7, 2022
Successful Launch:February 10, 2023 (SSLV-D2)
Launch Vehicle Type:Three-stage solid-fueled with a liquid-fueled VTM (Velocity Trimming Module)
Payload Capacity:500 kg to Low Earth Orbit (LEO), 300 kg to Sun-Synchronous Orbit (SSO)
Height:34 meters
Mass:120 tons
Launch Site:Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC), Sriharikota
Mission Duration:~15 minutes to orbit
Purpose:On-demand, low-cost launch solution for small satellites
Status:Operational
