Explorer 1 – America’s First Satellite

Explorer 1 was the first satellite launched by the United States on January 31, 1958, during the International Geophysical Year. Developed by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), it marked the country’s entry into the Space Race.

The satellite carried a cosmic ray detector and made the groundbreaking discovery of the Van Allen radiation belts. It remained operational for nearly four months before falling silent on May 23, 1958.

Specifications:

Launch Date:January 31, 1958
Launch Vehicle:Juno I
Mass:13.37 kg
Orbit:Low Earth Orbit (LEO)
Instruments:Cosmic Ray Detector
