NEW HORIZONS – FIRST PLUTO FLYBY (2015)

NASA's New Horizons spacecraft made its historic flyby of Pluto on July 14, 2015, capturing the first detailed images of the dwarf planet and its moons. It revealed unexpected geological activity, including ice mountains and a heart-shaped glacier called Sputnik Planitia. Launched in 2006, New Horizons traveled over 3 billion miles to reach Pluto, making it the fastest spacecraft ever launched. After its Pluto encounter, the mission was extended to explore other Kuiper Belt objects, such as Arrokoth in 2019. The mission dramatically changed our understanding of Pluto and the outer Solar System.
