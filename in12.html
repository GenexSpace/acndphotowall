A Satellite on a Bullock Cart

In 1981, ISRO made international headlines when it transported its first indigenously made geostationary communication satellite, APPLE (Ariane Passenger Payload Experiment), on a bullock cart. This unusual method was used to avoid magnetic interference during antenna alignment testing, highlighting the ingenuity of Indian scientists working under resource constraints.

Despite its humble beginnings, APPLE was successfully launched aboard the Ariane rocket and helped validate technologies used in later INSAT satellites. This iconic moment became a symbol of India’s ability to innovate with limited means and has since become an enduring image of the early Indian space program.

Specifications:

Launch Date:June 19, 1981
Launch Vehicle:Ariane 1
Launch Site:Kourou Spaceport, French Guiana
Mass:672 kg (at launch)
Orbit:Geostationary Transfer Orbit (GTO)
Mission Type:Experimental Communication Satellite
Payload:Two C-band transponders
Developed By:ISRO
Structure:Spin-stabilized, cylindrical body
Notable Fact:Famously transported on a bullock cart for antenna alignment testing
