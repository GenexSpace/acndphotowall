RLV-TD – India’s Space Shuttle Prototype (2016)

The Reusable Launch Vehicle–Technology Demonstrator (RLV-TD) program is ISRO's effort to develop a cost-effective and reusable space shuttle system. The prototype, named 'Pushpak', successfully completed its Hypersonic Flight Experiment on May 23, 2016, validating key technologies including autonomous navigation, thermal protection, and re-entry systems.

The RLV-TD test flight demonstrated India's entry into reusable space systems. Ongoing developments under this program are aimed at reducing satellite launch costs and achieving multiple reuses of a single vehicle platform.

Specifications:

Test Flight Date:May 23, 2016
Launch Vehicle:HS9 Solid Rocket Booster
Mission Type:Technology Demonstration (Suborbital)
Length:6.5 meters
Mass:1,750 kg
Features:Autonomous landing, reentry control, thermal shield
