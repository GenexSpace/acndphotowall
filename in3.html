Mangalyaan – First Mars Mission (2014)

The Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), also known as Mangalyaan, was launched on November 5, 2013, and successfully entered Martian orbit on September 24, 2014. It was developed with a remarkably low budget and made India the first nation to reach Mars on its maiden attempt.

Mangalyaan carried five instruments to study Martian surface, morphology, atmosphere, and mineralogy. It transmitted valuable data for nearly eight years and showcased India’s prowess in cost-effective, interplanetary mission design and execution.

Specifications:

Launch Date:November 5, 2013
Launch Vehicle:PSLV-C25
Orbit:Mars Orbit
Mass:1337 kg
