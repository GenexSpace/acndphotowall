SpaDeX – Docking in Space

SpaDeX (Space Docking Experiment) is an ISRO mission under development to demonstrate autonomous docking, proximity operations, and formation flying in space. The mission consists of twin satellites that will approach each other, hold relative position, and attempt a dock under full autonomous control.

This mission is critical for future human spaceflight, satellite servicing, and space station development. SpaDeX will validate rendezvous sensors, relative navigation algorithms, and docking mechanisms developed indigenously.

Specifications:

Mission Name:SpaDeX (Space Docking Experiment)
Launch Date:December 30, 2024
Launch Vehicle:PSLV-CA (C60)
Launch Site:Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota
Spacecraft:SDX01 (Chaser) and SDX02 (Target)
Spacecraft Mass:220 kg each
Orbit:475 km circular, 55° inclination
Mission Type:Autonomous Rendezvous, Docking, and Undocking
Docking System:Bhartiya Docking System (BDS), 450 mm interface
Key Technologies:V-bar approach, inter-satellite communication link, laser rangefinders, AI-based navigation
Power Transfer:Demonstrated between docked satellites
Undocking Date:March 13, 2025
Significance:India became the 4th nation to achieve autonomous in-space docking
