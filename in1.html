Aryabhata – India’s First Satellite (1975)

Launched on April 19, 1975, Aryabhata marked India’s debut into the space age. It was India’s first indigenously built satellite, designed and developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and launched by the Soviet Union from Kapustin Yar. The satellite, named after the ancient Indian mathematician and astronomer, was built for conducting experiments in X-ray astronomy and solar physics.

Although Aryabhata lost communication after just five days in orbit, it represented a monumental step in India’s space capability development. The mission provided ISRO with critical experience in satellite fabrication, systems integration, and space operations—laying the groundwork for future satellite missions.

Specifications:

Launch Date:April 19, 1975
Launch Vehicle:Kosmos-3M (USSR)
Orbit:Low Earth Orbit
Mass:360 kg
