Earth’s First Snapshot from Space (1946)

On October 24, 1946, a V-2 rocket launched from White Sands, New Mexico, carried a 35mm motion picture camera that captured the first photographs of Earth from space. These images, taken at altitudes exceeding 100 kilometers, revealed Earth's curvature for the first time in history.

The mission was part of Project Hermes, a post-WWII effort involving captured German technology and American scientific innovation. It marked the beginning of space-based Earth observation, which today plays a vital role in weather forecasting, navigation, and environmental monitoring.
