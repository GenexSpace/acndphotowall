APPLE – A Satellite on a Bullock Cart

In 1981, ISRO's experimental satellite APPLE (Ariane Passenger Payload Experiment) made headlines not only for its technical importance but also for being transported on a bullock cart during antenna testing. The satellite demonstrated India’s capability in geostationary communication technology.

APPLE was launched by an Ariane rocket from French Guiana and successfully relayed television signals. It laid the groundwork for India’s future communication satellite programs, especially the INSAT series.

Specifications:

Launch Date:June 19, 1981
Launch Site:Kourou, French Guiana
Function:Experimental GEO communication
