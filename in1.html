Aryabhata – India’s First Satellite (1975)

Launched on April 19, 1975, Aryabhata marked a monumental achievement in India's space program as the nation's first indigenously developed satellite. Built by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and launched from the Soviet Union aboard a Kosmos-3M launch vehicle, Aryabhata was designed for conducting experiments in X-ray astronomy, solar physics, and aeronomy.

Although it experienced power failure shortly after reaching orbit, Aryabhata symbolized India's entry into the global space community and laid the foundation for future advancements in satellite technology and engineering. It was named after the 5th-century Indian mathematician and astronomer Aryabhata.

Specifications:

Launch Date:April 19, 1975
Launch Vehicle:Kosmos-3M (Soviet Union)
Launch Site:Kapustin Yar, USSR
Mass:360 kg
Orbit:Low Earth Orbit (LEO)
Purpose:X-ray astronomy, solar studies, atmospheric science
