MANGALYAAN – FIRST MARS MISSION (2014)

The Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan), launched on November 5, 2013, became India's first interplanetary mission and the world's most cost-effective Mars mission. It entered Martian orbit on September 24, 2014, and provided valuable data on Mars' atmosphere and surface features. It operated for over seven years. Orbital Details:
- Mars Orbit: Elliptical (423 x 80,000 km)
- Inclination: 150°
- Orbital Period: ~72 hours
Technical Specifications:
- Launch Mass: 1,337 kg
- Power: 840 W solar panels
- Communication: 2.2 m high-gain antenna
- Payloads: Methane Sensor, Mars Color Camera, Thermal IR Spectrometer, Lyman Alpha Photometer
