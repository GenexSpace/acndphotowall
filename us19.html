WAC CORPORAL – FIRST US SOUNDING ROCKET (1945)

The WAC Corporal was the United States’ first sounding rocket, developed in 1945 by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL). It marked an early and important step in the nation’s journey into space.

Designed for high-altitude atmospheric research, the WAC Corporal was capable of reaching the edge of space and served as a vital testbed for rocket technology. It provided critical data about the upper atmosphere and paved the way for future missile and space launch vehicle development.

The rocket was part of the ORDCIT program (Ordnance-CIT), a collaboration between the U.S. Army and the California Institute of Technology. Its legacy lies in the foundational knowledge it contributed to American rocketry and space science.

Specifications and Facts:

First Launch:September 26, 1945
Developed By:Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL)
Height:4.88 m (16 ft)
Diameter:0.3 m (12 in)
Launch Sites:White Sands Missile Range
Significance:First US sounding rocket to reach edge of space
