New Horizons – First Pluto Flyby (2015)

Launched on January 19, 2006, New Horizons is a NASA mission developed to perform the first reconnaissance of Pluto and its moons. The spacecraft completed its historic flyby of Pluto on July 14, 2015, providing humanity with its first close-up views of the dwarf planet’s complex surface features and atmosphere.

Following its primary mission, New Horizons continued its journey into the Kuiper Belt and successfully performed a flyby of the distant object Arrokoth in 2019. The spacecraft remains active and continues to send data back to Earth, expanding our understanding of the outer solar system.

Specifications:

Launch:2006
Pluto Flyby:2015
Kuiper Object:Arrokoth (2019)
