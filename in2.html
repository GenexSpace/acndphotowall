Chandrayaan-1 – Discovery of Water on the Moon (2008)

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first mission to the Moon, launched on October 22, 2008, from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre aboard a PSLV-C11 rocket. The orbiter carried 11 scientific instruments from multiple countries and was pivotal in confirming the presence of water molecules on the lunar surface, a major breakthrough in planetary science.

The mission operated until August 2009. Its success established ISRO as a serious player in deep space exploration and significantly contributed to global lunar science.

Specifications:

Launch Date:October 22, 2008
Launch Vehicle:PSLV-C11
Launch Site:Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC), Sriharikota
Mission Type:Lunar Orbiter
Mass:1,380 kg
Orbit:Polar Lunar Orbit (100 km circular)
Instruments:11 instruments (ISRO, NASA, ESA, Bulgaria)
Key Discovery:Confirmed presence of water molecules on the Moon
Operational Duration:10 months (planned: 2 years)
