MAN ON THE MOON - APOLLO 12 MISSION (1969)

The Apollo 12 mission, launched on November 14, 1969, was NASA's second crewed Moon landing, and it further demonstrated humanity's capability to explore extraterrestrial worlds.

Astronaut Alan Bean became the fourth human to walk on the Moon, capturing crucial photographs and collecting lunar soil and rock samples for scientific study. In one notable image from the mission, astronaut Charles "Pete" Conrad can be seen reflected in Bean's visor, taking a photograph with a chest-mounted camera similar to Bean’s own.

Apollo 12 provided valuable scientific insights and proved the precision landing capabilities required for future lunar missions. The astronauts explored the lunar surface extensively, spending over 31 hours outside the spacecraft during two extravehicular activities (EVAs).

Specifications and Facts:

Mission Launch Date:November 14, 1969
Landing Site:Ocean of Storms (Oceanus Procellarum)
Commander:Charles "Pete" Conrad
Lunar Module Pilot:Alan L. Bean
Duration on Moon:31 hours, 31 minutes
Lunar Samples Collected:34.35 kilograms (75.7 pounds)
