Nike-Apache – Early Rocket Development (1963)

The Nike-Apache sounding rocket marked India’s entry into experimental rocketry and space-based atmospheric research. Launched on November 21, 1963, from the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS), it was a two-stage sounding rocket provided by NASA under a scientific collaboration initiative.

This launch laid the foundation for India’s indigenous rocket development, leading to programs like the Rohini Sounding Rocket and the later SLV series. The Nike-Apache mission symbolized the humble beginnings of what would become a robust and world-recognized space program.

Specifications:

Launch Date:November 21, 1963
Launch Site:Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS)
Rocket Type:Two-stage sounding rocket (Nike + Apache)
Purpose:Atmospheric and ionospheric research
Collaborators:NASA, INCOSPAR
Significance:First sounding rocket launch from Indian soil
