ASTROSAT – India’s Space Observatory (2015)

ASTROSAT, launched in September 2015, is India’s first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory. It observes celestial objects in visible, ultraviolet, and X-ray spectrums simultaneously.

The mission has enabled major contributions in astrophysics, including studies of black holes, neutron stars, and galaxy formation, and has positioned India in the league of space observatories like NASA's Chandra and ESA's XMM-Newton.

Specifications:

Launch Date:September 28, 2015
Orbit:Near-Earth Orbit
Spectrum:X-ray, UV, Optical
