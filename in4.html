Chandrayaan-3 – Soft Landing Near the Moon’s South Pole (2023)

Chandrayaan-3 was India's second attempt at a lunar soft landing and achieved a historic milestone by successfully landing near the Moon’s south pole on August 23, 2023. The mission showcased India's growing proficiency in autonomous landing technologies and lunar surface operations. It consisted of a lander named Vikram and a rover named Pragyan, designed to explore the surface and conduct scientific studies.

The south polar region of the Moon, targeted for its potential water ice and unique geology, became accessible for the first time through this mission. Chandrayaan-3's success made India the first country to land in this region, enhancing its status in the global space community.

Specifications:

Launch Date:July 14, 2023
Landing Date:August 23, 2023
Launch Vehicle:LVM3-M4
Mission Type:Lunar Lander and Rover
Landing Site:Moon’s South Polar Region (~69.37°S, 32.35°E)
Lander:Vikram
Rover:Pragyan (6-wheeled solar-powered)
Mass:3,900 kg (composite)
Instruments:RAMBHA-LP, ChaSTE, ILSA, APXS, LIBS
Significance:First spacecraft to land near lunar south pole
