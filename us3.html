VOYAGER 1 & 2 – EXPLORING THE OUTER SOLAR SYSTEM (1977)

Voyager 1 and 2 were launched in 1977 as part of NASA’s effort to explore the outer planets of our Solar System. These spacecraft delivered the first detailed images and data from Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Voyager 1, now the farthest human-made object from Earth, captured the iconic Pale Blue Dot image of Earth from 6 billion kilometers away, offering a humbling perspective of our planet. Voyager 2 remains the only spacecraft to have visited all four gas giants.

Both spacecraft are now in interstellar space, continuing to send back data and carrying the Golden Record — a time capsule of Earth’s culture and sounds — for any extraterrestrial life that may one day encounter it.

Specifications and Facts:

Launch Year:1977
Primary Targets:Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus (Voyager 2), Neptune (Voyager 2)
Status:Active – in Interstellar Space
Famous Image:Pale Blue Dot (Voyager 1)
Onboard Message:Golden Record (music, greetings, images)
