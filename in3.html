Mangalyaan – First Mars Mission (2014)

Launched on November 5, 2013, Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission) was India’s first interplanetary mission and made global headlines when it successfully entered Martian orbit on September 24, 2014. This mission demonstrated ISRO's cost-effective engineering, achieving the feat on a budget of around $74 million, one of the lowest ever for a Mars mission.

The spacecraft carried five scientific instruments to study Mars' surface features, morphology, atmosphere, and mineralogy. India became the first country to succeed in a Mars mission on its first attempt, marking a significant milestone in space exploration.

Specifications:

Launch Date:November 5, 2013
Launch Vehicle:PSLV-XL (C25)
Mass:1,337 kg
Orbit:Highly Elliptical Mars Orbit
Instruments:Mars Colour Camera, Methane Sensor, Thermal IR Spectrometer, MENCA
Mission Duration:Planned: 6 months, Extended: Over 7 years
