HAM – THE FIRST HOMINID IN SPACE (1961)

On January 31, 1961, a chimpanzee named Ham became the first hominid to travel into space, paving the way for human spaceflight. He flew aboard the Mercury-Redstone 2 capsule as part of NASA's Project Mercury, designed to test whether living beings could survive and perform tasks in space.

Ham was trained to push levers in response to light cues, proving that astronauts could work in microgravity. The mission lasted just over 16 minutes and reached an altitude of 252,000 feet (approximately 76.8 km). Although the capsule landed slightly off course in the Atlantic Ocean, Ham was recovered safely and became an iconic symbol of early space exploration.

His successful flight provided the confidence needed to move forward with the first human spaceflights, including Alan Shepard’s suborbital mission just a few months later.

Specifications and Facts:

Flight Date:January 31, 1961
Species:Chimpanzee (Pan troglodytes)
Mission:Mercury-Redstone 2
Duration:16.5 minutes
Peak Altitude:252,000 feet (76.8 km)
Legacy:Paved the way for human spaceflight
