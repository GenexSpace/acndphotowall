GSLV – POWERING HEAVY PAYLOADS INTO SPACE

The Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV) is India’s key rocket for launching heavy payloads into geostationary orbit.

It supports missions involving communication, navigation, and Earth observation. GSLV can carry payloads up to 2,500 kg to GTO and 5,000 kg to LEO.

Technical Highlights:


- Payload to GTO: 2,500 kg


- Payload to LEO: 5,000 kg


- Stages: Three-stage rocket with a cryogenic upper stage


- Missions: GSAT series, Chandrayaan-2


Technical Specifications:

  • Stages: 3 (Solid + Liquid + Cryogenic)
  • Height: 49 m
  • Diameter: 3.4 m
  • Liftoff Mass: ~415,000 kg
  • Engine: CE-7.5 Cryogenic Engine (Upper stage)
  • Thrust: 4700 kN (core stage)
