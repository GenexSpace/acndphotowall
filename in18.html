Dr. Vikram Sarabhai – Architect of India's Space Program

Dr. Vikram Sarabhai is revered as the father of the Indian space program. A visionary physicist and institution-builder, he founded the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969, laying the groundwork for the country's scientific and technological advancement in space. Sarabhai championed the use of space for societal development, emphasizing its role in communication, meteorology, and education.

Under his leadership, India launched its first sounding rockets and established the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS). His legacy includes the creation of key institutions like the Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad (IIM-A), and space-science education networks across India.

Specifications:

Born:August 12, 1919
Founded ISRO:1969
Key Roles:PRL Founder, IIM-A Co-founder, Space Visionary
Notable Initiatives:TERLS, Satellite Instructional TV Experiment (SITE)
Honors:Padma Bhushan, Padma Vibhushan
Legacy:Inspired India’s peaceful space development ethos
