CHANDRAYAAN-1 – DISCOVERY OF WATER ON THE MOON (2008)

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first mission to the Moon, launched on October 22, 2008, aboard a PSLV-XL rocket from Sriharikota. It discovered water molecules on the Moon and created high-resolution maps using instruments from India, NASA, ESA, and Bulgaria. The Moon Impact Probe (MIP) carried the Indian flag to the Moon’s surface. Orbital Details:
- Initial Orbit: Earth Transfer Orbit
- Lunar Orbit: 100 km circular polar orbit
- Inclination: ~90°
- Duration: 312 days (ended August 2009)
Technical Specifications:
- Mass: 1,380 kg at launch
- Power: 750 W solar arrays
- Instruments: Terrain Mapping Camera, Moon Mineralogy Mapper (M3), X-ray Spectrometer, and others
