Longest Female Spaceflight – Sunita Williams

Sunita Lyn Williams is a NASA astronaut of Indian-Slovenian heritage who has spent a cumulative 322 days in space across two missions aboard the ISS. She holds the record for the most spacewalk time by a woman (over 50 hours), performed during expeditions 14, 15, and 32.

Williams, a former U.S. Navy test pilot, continues to inspire young people worldwide. She is part of NASA’s Commercial Crew Program and is expected to fly aboard the Boeing CST-100 Starliner. Her contributions symbolize endurance, diversity, and leadership in human spaceflight.

About Her:

Full Name:Sunita Lyn Williams
Date of Birth:September 19, 1965
Nationality:American (of Indian-Slovenian descent)
First Spaceflight:December 9, 2006 (STS-116 / Expedition 14/15)
Total Time in Space:322 days (over two missions)
EVAs (Spacewalks):7 EVAs totaling 50 hours 40 minutes
Notable Records:Former record-holder for most spacewalk time by a woman
Current Role:NASA astronaut; selected pilot for Boeing Starliner CFT mission
