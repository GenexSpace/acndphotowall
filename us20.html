WERNHER VON BRAUN – PIONEER OF ROCKETRY

Wernher von Braun was one of the most influential rocket engineers in history. Born in Germany in 1912, he led the development of the V-2 rocket during World War II. After the war, von Braun was brought to the United States under Operation Paperclip, where he played a critical role in the U.S. space program.

As the director of NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center, he was the chief architect of the Saturn V rocket, which successfully carried Apollo astronauts to the Moon. His vision and leadership turned the dream of lunar exploration into a reality and laid the foundation for modern space exploration.

Von Braun was a passionate advocate for space science and education, inspiring generations to look toward the stars. His legacy lives on in every rocket that ventures beyond our planet.

Biography Highlights:

Born:March 23, 1912 – Wirsitz, Germany
Died:June 16, 1977 – Alexandria, Virginia, USA
Key Contributions:V-2 Rocket, Saturn V, Apollo Program
NASA Role:Director, Marshall Space Flight Center (1960–1970)
Legacy:Architect of Apollo Moon missions
