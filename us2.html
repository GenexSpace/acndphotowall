Apollo 11 – First Moon Landing (1969)

Apollo 11 was the culmination of the United States’ effort to land a man on the Moon and return him safely to Earth, fulfilling President John F. Kennedy's ambitious 1961 goal. Managed by NASA and executed through the Apollo program, the mission launched on July 16, 1969, aboard the Saturn V rocket from Kennedy Space Center, Florida. Astronauts Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins comprised the crew, with Armstrong and Aldrin destined for the lunar surface while Collins remained in lunar orbit.

On July 20, 1969, the Lunar Module 'Eagle' touched down on the Moon in the Sea of Tranquility, and Neil Armstrong took humanity’s first step onto another world, uttering the iconic words: “That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind.” Aldrin soon followed, conducting scientific experiments and deploying instruments. Apollo 11 safely returned to Earth on July 24, marking a landmark achievement in space exploration and human history.

Specifications:

Launch Date:July 16, 1969
Lunar Landing:July 20, 1969
Crew:Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, Michael Collins
Landing Site:Sea of Tranquility
Return Date:July 24, 1969
