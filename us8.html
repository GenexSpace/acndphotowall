James Webb Space Telescope (2021)

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is NASA’s next-generation flagship space observatory launched on December 25, 2021. A collaboration between NASA, ESA, and CSA, JWST is designed to observe the universe in infrared wavelengths, enabling astronomers to peer deeper into space and time than ever before.

Positioned at the second Lagrange point (L2), 1.5 million kilometers from Earth, JWST features a 6.5-meter gold-coated mirror and a sunshield the size of a tennis court. It has begun delivering groundbreaking imagery of galaxies, star formation, and exoplanet atmospheres, redefining our view of the cosmos.

Specifications:

Mirror Size:6.5 meters
Orbit:L2 (1.5M km)
