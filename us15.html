Starship – The Future of Space Travel

Starship is a fully reusable launch vehicle system developed by SpaceX for missions to low Earth orbit, the Moon, Mars, and beyond. Comprising two stages—Super Heavy booster and Starship spacecraft—it is designed to deliver over 100 metric tons to orbit.

Starship is central to NASA’s Artemis program and is intended to enable human settlement on other worlds. It is currently undergoing extensive testing at SpaceX's Boca Chica facility in Texas, with a focus on orbital launches and landing precision.

Specifications:

First Integrated Flight:April 20, 2023
Height:120 meters (394 feet)
Diameter:9 meters
Mass (Fully Fueled):~5,000,000 kg
Payload to LEO:150,000–250,000 kg (fully reusable)
Stages:2 (Super Heavy booster + Starship upper stage)
Engines:33 Raptor (booster), 6 Raptor (upper stage)
Propellant:Liquid Methane + Liquid Oxygen (CH₄/LOX)
Reusability:Fully reusable (both stages)
Applications:Lunar missions, Mars transport, Earth-to-Earth cargo
Status:In development and active flight testing (as of 2025)
