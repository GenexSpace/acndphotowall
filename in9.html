INSAT – Transforming India’s Communication (1983–Present)

The Indian National Satellite System (INSAT), launched in 1983, revolutionized India’s communication, meteorology, and disaster alert infrastructure. With satellites like INSAT-1B, which provided telecommunications, television broadcasting, and weather forecasting services, the program helped bridge the urban-rural communication gap and enabled real-time cyclone warnings and emergency communications.

Over the years, INSAT has evolved with advanced payloads, supporting DTH broadcasting, VSAT networks, and satellite-based education (EDUSAT). INSAT remains a cornerstone of India’s geostationary satellite capabilities, continuously monitored and managed by ISRO’s Master Control Facilities.

Specifications:

Program Start:1983
First Operational Satellite:INSAT-1B
Launch Date (INSAT-1B):August 30, 1983
Orbit:Geostationary Orbit (~36,000 km)
Applications:Telecommunications, TV Broadcasting, Meteorology, Disaster Alerts, Search and Rescue
Operators:ISRO and Department of Space
Satellite Series:INSAT-1, INSAT-2, INSAT-3, INSAT-4
Total Satellites Launched:Over 20
Collaborations:NASA, ISRO, EADS Astrium, and others
Status:Operational (latest satellites co-located with GSAT series)
