Chandrayaan-3 – Landing Near the Moon’s South Pole (2023)

Chandrayaan-3 was launched on July 14, 2023, and successfully achieved a soft landing near the Moon’s south pole on August 23, making India the first nation to do so. The mission included a lander named Vikram and a rover named Pragyan.

Unlike its predecessor, Chandrayaan-3 focused entirely on demonstrating a safe and soft landing on the lunar surface. The mission achieved all of its goals, including traversing the terrain and conducting elemental analysis, placing India among the top lunar-exploring nations.

Specifications:

Launch Date:July 14, 2023
Launch Vehicle:LVM3-M4
Landing:August 23, 2023
