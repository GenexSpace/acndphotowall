ARYABHATA – INDIA’S FIRST SATELLITE (1975)

Aryabhata was India's first satellite, launched on April 19, 1975, from the Soviet Union aboard a Kosmos-3M rocket. It marked India’s entry into the global space community. The satellite carried instruments to study X-ray astronomy, solar physics, and aeronomy. Though it lost power after five days, it transmitted useful data and demonstrated India's satellite design capabilities. Orbital Details:
- Orbit Type: Low Earth Orbit (LEO)
- Inclination: ~50.7°
- Altitude: ~600 km
- Orbital Period: ~96 minutes
Technical Specifications:
- Shape: 26-sided polygon
- Mass: 360 kg
- Power: Solar panels and Ni-Cd batteries
- Payloads: X-ray and Solar detectors
