Astrosat – India’s Space Observatory (2015)

Astrosat, launched on September 28, 2015, is India’s first multi-wavelength space observatory designed to observe cosmic sources in visible, ultraviolet, and X-ray bands. It marked ISRO’s entry into the domain of space-based astronomy and enabled detailed studies of stellar evolution, black holes, and distant galaxies.

Equipped with instruments like the UVIT and LAXPC, Astrosat provided unprecedented opportunities for Indian astronomers and global collaborations. It placed India among a select group of nations with dedicated space telescopes.

Specifications:

Launch Date:September 28, 2015
Launch Vehicle:PSLV-C30
Orbit:Low Earth Orbit (650 km, 6° inclination)
Mass:1,513 kg
Instruments:UVIT, SXT, LAXPC, CZTI, SSM
Mission Type:Astronomical Observatory
