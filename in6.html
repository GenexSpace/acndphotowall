Cartosat – Advancing Earth Observation

The Cartosat series consists of Earth observation satellites developed by ISRO for cartographic applications, urban and rural planning, and infrastructure development. Equipped with high-resolution panchromatic and multispectral cameras, these satellites provide detailed imagery that supports environmental monitoring and disaster management.

Since 2005, several Cartosat satellites have been launched, each improving spatial resolution and imaging frequency. The data from Cartosat is extensively used in defense, agriculture, water resource management, and topographic mapping.

Specifications:

First Launch:May 5, 2005
Latest Satellite:Cartosat-3 (November 27, 2019)
Orbit:Sun-Synchronous Polar Orbit (~500–650 km)
Payload:Panchromatic and Multispectral Cameras
Resolution:Up to 0.25 m (Cartosat-3)
Application:Cartography, disaster management, urban planning
