NIKE-APACHE – EARLY ROCKET DEVELOPMENT (1963)

The Nike-Apache was one of India’s earliest sounding rockets. It was used for atmospheric and space research and laid the foundation for India’s future launch vehicles.

Its first launch marked a pivotal point in India’s entry into space research.

Mission Details:


- First Launch: 1963 from Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS)


- Purpose: Scientific sounding missions


- Contribution: Training and international collaboration


Technical Specifications:

  • Height: 8.38 m (combined Nike + Apache)
  • Apogee: ~200 km
  • Payload: Atmospheric instruments
  • Origin: Nike booster from USA + Apache upper stage
